SECTION 1: OVERVIEW
The FIFA World Cup 2026 is the first men's World Cup with 48 teams. It is co-hosted by Canada, Mexico, and the United States. The tournament runs from 11 June 2026 to 19 July 2026 and contains 104 matches.

The format is 12 groups of 4 teams. The top two teams from each group, plus the eight best third-placed teams, advance to a 32-team knockout stage.

Opening match: Mexico vs South Africa, Estadio Azteca / Mexico City Stadium, Mexico City, 11 June 2026.
Final: MetLife Stadium / New York New Jersey Stadium, East Rutherford, New Jersey, 19 July 2026.

SECTION 5: QUALIFICATION CRITERIA AND SLOT ALLOCATION
The 2026 World Cup expanded from 32 to 48 teams. Canada, Mexico, and the United States qualified automatically as hosts.

Confederation slot allocation:
- AFC: 8 direct spots plus 1 inter-confederation playoff spot
- CAF: 9 direct spots plus 1 inter-confederation playoff spot
- CONCACAF: 6 direct spots including the 3 host nations, plus 2 inter-confederation playoff spots
- CONMEBOL: 6 direct spots plus 1 inter-confederation playoff spot
- OFC: 1 direct spot plus 1 inter-confederation playoff spot
- UEFA: 16 direct spots

Inter-confederation playoffs:
- Six teams competed for the final two World Cup places.
- The playoff field included one team each from AFC, CAF, CONMEBOL, and OFC, plus two teams from CONCACAF.
- The playoff tournament was held in Mexico in March 2026.

SECTION 6: TOURNAMENT FORMAT
Group stage:
- 12 groups of 4 teams.
- Each team plays 3 group matches.
- Win = 3 points.
- Draw = 1 point.
- Loss = 0 points.
- The top two teams in each group advance.
- The eight best third-placed teams also advance.

Knockout stage:
- 32 teams enter the knockout stage.
- Rounds: Round of 32, Round of 16, Quarter-finals, Semi-finals, Third-place match, Final.
- Knockout matches are single elimination.
- If a knockout match is tied after 90 minutes, 30 minutes of extra time is played.
- If still tied after extra time, the winner is decided by a penalty shootout.

SECTION 7: GROUP-STAGE TIEBREAKERS
The standard FIFA group ranking logic is:
1. Points in all group matches
2. Goal difference in all group matches
3. Goals scored in all group matches
4. Points in matches between tied teams
5. Goal difference in matches between tied teams
6. Goals scored in matches between tied teams
7. Fair play points / team conduct score
8. Drawing of lots by FIFA

Fair play deductions:
- Yellow card: -1 point
- Indirect red card / second yellow: -3 points
- Direct red card: -4 points
- Yellow card plus direct red card: -5 points
